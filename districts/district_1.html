Nasugbu City, Batangas

Nasugbu is a coastal town that was founded in 1581. It was an important site for trade and commerce during the Spanish colonial period, serving as a major port for shipping agricultural products such as coffee and sugar. Nasugbu was also a significant site during World War II, as it was the location of several battles between Filipino and Japanese forces. Today, Nasugbu is a popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches and scenic landscapes.

Discover the beauty of Nasugbu, Batangas, a hidden treasure waiting to be discovered. Nasugbu offers an amazing travel experience with its gorgeous scenery, pristine beaches, and stunning natural treasures. Discover Fortune Island's secret gem, where crystal-clear seas and towering cliffs form an unearthly scene. Swim in the turquoise seas, explore the remains of Greek-inspired architecture, or simply relax in the sun and take in the breathtaking vistas. Hiking atop the beautiful Mount Batulao will reward daring hearts with a panoramic view of Batangas and Cavite. For those who enjoy the sea, the Hamilo Coast provides a magnificent beach experience unrivalled anyplace else. Swim in the warm waves, lounge on the white sand beach, or participate in water activities such as kayaking, snorkelling, and paddleboarding.

Nasugbu, nevertheless is beyond just natural wonders. It's also a cultural and historical hotspot, with sites and institutions that will transport you back in time. Explore the unique architecture of ancestral houses in Nasugbu town, or visit the historic Nasugbu Church, one of the province's oldest.

Discover the beauty of Nasugbu and make memories to last a lifetime. Nasugbu is the ideal destination for your next journey, with its magnificent landscapes, rich culture, and pristine beaches.

Fortune Island

Originally known as Batumbalani Island, the island was revered as a sacred location by the Tagalog people. After a group of investors constructed a resort on the island in the 1990s, the island was later given the new name Fortune Island. Due to financial issues, the resort, which was intended to be an opulent retreat for the wealthy and privileged, was short-lived.

The resort previously included the mansion with Greek-inspired ruins that are still visible on the island. The mansion was intended to be the focal point of the resort and was modelled after the Temple of Poseidon in Greece. The mansion, however, was never finished and was finally left unfinished.

Hamilo Coast

The construction of Hamilo Coast began in the late 1990s, when the SM Group, one of the Philippines' major conglomerates, purchased a 5,900-hectare property along Nasugbu's coastline and its development began in 2003, with a master plan that includes luxury villas, condominiums, hotels, and a variety of recreational and leisure facilities.

The SM Group envisioned a world-class beach resort and residential community that would highlight the area's natural beauty while simultaneously promoting sustainable tourism practices and benefiting the local population.

Mount Batulao

Mount Batulao is a popular hiking destination located in Nasugbu, Batangas, Philippines. The history of Mount Batulao dates back to pre-colonial times, when the mountain was considered a sacred site by the local Tagalog people, who believed it to be the dwelling place of spirits and deities.

During the Spanish colonial period, Mount Batulao became a refuge for local resistance fighters and revolutionaries who opposed Spanish rule. The mountain's rugged terrain and remote location made it an ideal location for guerrilla warfare. In more recent times, Mount Batulao has become a popular hiking destination, attracting visitors from across the Philippines and beyond. The mountain's well-maintained trails, stunning views, and challenging terrain make it a favorite among both experienced hikers and beginners.
