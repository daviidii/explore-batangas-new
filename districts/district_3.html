Tanauan City, Batangas

Tanauan, Batangas, is a lovely town that is full of surprises. Learn about its hidden beauty! Tanauan offers everything you could possibly want for a great holiday, from its rich history to its natural beauty. Visit the town's well-known attractions, such the Our Lady of Rosary Parish Church and marvel at its complex architecture, and learn the tales behind its cultural legacy.

If you enjoy the outdoors, you'll be happy to learn that Tanauan is home to a number of magnificent natural beauties. Enjoy the breathtaking views of the town and surroundings by hiking up Mount Makiling. Visit Marian Orchard, a heavenly place that provides a welcome respite from the city's bustle. Alternately, you can enjoy the healing effects of nature by swimming in a hot spring or waterfall nearby.

And Tanauan won't let you down when it comes to the food. Enjoy the culinary highlights of Batangas, including the bulalo, lomi, and adobo sa dilaw. Additionally, you can stroll through the town's bustling markets and find tasty treats and mementos to take home. Tanauan is the ideal location for anyone seeking either a tranquil getaway or an exhilarating experience.

Marian Orchard

Marian Orchard is a Catholic pilgrimage destination in the Philippine town of Balete, Tanauan City, Batangas. The site is dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary and contains a diverse collection of plants, flowers, and fruit trees.

Marian Orchard's history begins in the early 2000s, when the land was purchased by a group of Catholic laypeople with the purpose of establishing a place of prayer and thought. The Friends of the Divine Mercy group began planting fruit trees and flowers on the site, inspired by St. Therese of Lisieux's teachings on the value of doing modest acts of kindness and charity.

Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary Parish Chruch

The Tanauan Church was built during the Spanish colonial period in 1750. The Augustinian friars, who were in charge of evangelism in the area, oversaw the construction of the church. The church was finished in 1770 and was regarded as one of the most magnificent in the Philippines at the time.

The original church was made of adobe stones, but it was destroyed by an earthquake in 1849. The church was then rebuilt using stronger materials, such as bricks and tiles, and was completed in 1856. However, the church was once again destroyed by a strong earthquake in 1942, and it took several years to rebuild it. The church was finally reconstructed in 1949, and it still stands today as a testament to the perseverance and faith of the Tanauan community.

Mount Makiling

Mount Makiling is a dormant volcano in the provinces of Laguna and Batangas on the Philippine island of Luzon. The mountain is named after Maria Makiling, a legendary character who is claimed to reside in the mountain and protect its woods and wildlife.

Mount Makiling has a long history and has served as an important place for both cultural and scientific research. Archaeological findings indicate that the mountain was occupied by early human settlements as early as 5,000 years ago, and the indigenous Tagalog people felt it was a sacred spot where their ancestors resided.
